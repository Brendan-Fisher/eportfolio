Journal

September 18, 2019

When I first heard about the e-portfolio assignment that was required by the URSP program, I was intimidated, I didn't know what to do, where to start. As a computer science student, however, I was excited. I was interested in creating my own website, something that I could truly call my own, a project that summarized my interests, my classes, and what makes me me. I immediately knew that I wouldn't be going the route of wixsites or weebly and knew that I had to create something of my own imagination, something that I could truly call my own. "What kind of computer science student would I be," I thought to myself, if I couldn't even create my own website. And so I did, with the help of only Youtube videos, Google searches, and much trial and error, I made this portfolio from the ground up. Comprised of elements of HTML5, CSS, Javascript, and other components, I am proud to call this website my own, my first true project.
