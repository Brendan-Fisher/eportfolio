Journal & Photos

Let me tell you a few things...

Sept. 18, 2019

When I first heard about the e-portfolio assignment that was required by the URSP program, I was intimidated, I didn't know what to do, where to start. As a computer science student, however, I was excited. I was interested in creating my own website, something that I could truly call my own, a project that summarized my interests, my classes, and what makes me me. I immediately knew that I wouldn't be going the route of wixsites or weebly and knew that I had to create something of my own imagination, something that I could truly call my own. "What kind of computer science student would I be," I thought to myself, if I couldn't even create my own website. And so I did, with the help of only Youtube videos, Google searches, and much trial and error, I made this portfolio from the ground up. Comprised of elements of HTML5, CSS, Javascript, and other components, I am proud to call this website my own, my first true project.

Oct. 25, 2019

As the first half of the semester comes and goes, I'd like to take this opportunity to reflect on my time so far, what I've done, what I wished I would have done, and what I look forward to doing in the coming days and weeks. Classes have been good so far, for the first time in as long as I can remember, I'm actually enjoying what I'm learning and what I'm doing, whether it be the labs and projects in my programming fundamentals class or the egg drop design competition we did for the last day of my professional development class. Coming into this year I had many aspirations for getting involved in clubs, organizations, and other activities. In reality, I've come to realize that it's very difficult to balance a large courseload and all of these extracurriculars and as a result, I've not been as involved as I was planning on being. This is something that I am hoping to improve on in the latter half of this semester. Like many other students, I struggle with procrastination, however I know that I can overcome this and become the student that I want to be.
